MIT has been one of the more life-changing eras of my life. With that being said, there is an undeniable pressure cooker type of vibe that I have only felt more and more as more semesters pass. I'm constantly in awe of what my friends accomplish, which is immensely inspiring, but concurrently there is absolutely no space I feel left to breathe and to take a minute to think without the constant pressure of moving forward. It's hard, honestly, because I feel like my semesters have always been full of compromises. I compromise the time I spend and split between my commitments, or the decisions I make to try and pursue both spontaneity with friends and responsibility on my academics. For the majority of it, I also repeatedly feel so "behind the curve" -- behind some unspoken, hidden curve that others just know about and I never got the orientiation for. The career curve, the one where people seemingly knew exactly what to do and how to position themselves for summer internships.

Starting is the hardest, hardest part.
